FAQS

Is pepper spray legal to carry in India?

Yes. Pepper spray is commonly permitted for personal self-defence in India and does not require a licence under existing law. However, it must be used strictly for lawful self-defence. Misuse or use with intent to harm may attract legal consequences under applicable laws.

Disclaimer : Users are advised to comply with local regulations and use responsibly. This product is intended solely for personal safety.

Can I carry Nazr’s pepper spray in my bag or on a flight or train?

Nazr’s On Me clips to the outside of your bag or keychain for quick access. Pepper spray is not permitted in cabin baggage on flights in India and may be restricted even in checked baggage depending on airline and security policies. It is generally allowed on trains for personal safety, but must be carried and used responsibly in accordance with applicable rules. Policies may vary by airline or authority. Please check before travel

Is Nazr’s pepper spray safe to use indoors?

For outdoor use or well-ventilated spaces only. Avoid contact with your own eyes, skin, and face. This product contains OC (Oleoresin Capsicum), a widely used self-defence agent that causes temporary irritation, including burning sensation, tearing, and breathing discomfort. Use only in situations of legitimate self-defence. Effects are temporary but may cause discomfort. Individuals with respiratory conditions should exercise caution. Causes temporary eye closure, tearing, and disorientation lasting several minutes.
